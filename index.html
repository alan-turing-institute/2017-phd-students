Martin O'Reilly

Research Software Engineer

Research interests

Reproducible research; Cloud computing; Machine learning; Image processing; Neuroscience;

About me

After a BSc in Physics at Imperial and a year as a travelling salesman in Australia, I worked an analyst and software developer for 5 years, eventually running my own team. While enjoying my time in industry, I wanted to work on problems no one had solved before, so I went back to school - first to a master's in artificial intelligence at Edinburgh and then to a PhD in computational neuroscience at UCL.

Enjoying the research environment but frustrated with the slow pace, superspecialisation and precarious nature of the academic career path, I went back to industry for 3 years as an independent software development consultant. Last November, I discovered research software engineering and the Turing and now marry all my professional interests running the software engineering half of the Turing's Research Engineering team.

Amartya Sanyal

PhD student

Research interests

Learning Theory; Optimization; Deep Learning; Adversarial Models;

About me

Here is my personal webpage!. I have an undergraduate degree from IIT Kanpur with a B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering and a minor in Linguistics Theory. During these four years, I visited quite a few places where I carried out short term research projects. I spent some time in Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata and Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru. Following that I spent a summer at the Amazon ML lab in Bangalore where I worked on developing a model in competitive pricing, which was later deployed(supposedly). I spent the next summer in the Montreal Institute of Learning Algorithms(MILA) where I worked on developing an adaptive segmentation language generation model, which was published at the MLSLP workshop at ICML, Sydney. I spent the following summer at the Twitter Cortex group in NYC working with Nicholas Leonard and Nicolas Koumchatzky woking on adversarial discrete sequence generation models.

Currently, I am a doctoral student in the Department of Computer Science at The University of Oxford where I am supervised by Dr. Varun Kanade and Dr. Phil Torr from the Engineering Scienec department. I also active engage in coding and contributing to open source libraries, which is available in my github repository. I am also interested in Brazilian Ji-Jitsu and board games.
